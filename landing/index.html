Battlehack is a real-time strategy game played by code instead of people. Teams of virtual robots roam the screen, managing resources and executing different strategies. The game will be announced at the start of the hackathon.

Team up in groups of 1-4, and work together to program your bot. We'll give you all the food and swag you need, and a place to crash when you need a break. At the end of the hackathon, your code will be run in a tournament with all teams, and a winner will be declared.

FAQ

Battlewhat? What is this?

Battlehack is a 24 hour hackathon run by the people behind Battlecode, a 17 year old MIT AI programming competition.


What's a hackathon?

A hackathon is a fancy name for a competition, usually overnight, where a bunch of programmers work in teams writing code. Battlehack is special because instead of deciding their own projects, everybody works towards the same goal: writing bots for the Battlehack game.


Can I participate?

If you're a university student over 18, yes! Otherwise, unfortunately no.
